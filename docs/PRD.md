# Product Requirements Document: Revision of "How to Make a Complete Map of Every Thought You Think"

1. Introduction

1.1. Purpose of Revision

Modernize the book for contemporary tools and workflows, including large language models.

Enhance clarity and accessibility for a technical audience.

1.2. Goals

Increase readership and relevance among experienced note-takers and automated tools.

Provide a practical guide for thought mapping in the digital age, with a focus on adaptability for LLMs.

2. Target Audience

Experienced note-takers and automated tools, such as large language models.

3. Scope

3.1. Included Revisions

Modernize terminology and examples.

Integrate markdown for formatting.

Incorporate #tags for categorization.

Implement backlink functionality.

Explain the use of Entity-Relationship Diagrams (ERDs) or graph databases for thought mapping.

Update the bibliography and references.

3.2. Excluded Revisions

No major changes to the core methodology of thought mapping.

4. Functional Requirements

4.1. Content Updates

Replace references to paper, pen, and binders with digital equivalents.

Provide examples of markdown syntax for formatting notes.

Explain how to use #tags to categorize and organize thoughts.

Describe how to create and utilize backlinks, both within a digital book and in external note-taking systems.

Detail how ERDs or graph databases can be used to visualize and explore thought networks, with a focus on their potential for use with LLMs.

4.2. New Sections

Include a section on recommended software tools, emphasizing flexibility and extensibility.

Add a section on mindmaps and their relation to knowledge graphs

4.3. Output

Digital book in markdown format.
